abstract: "Intelligent Tutoring Systems are eﬀective at improving learning, but are costly to produce. In this paper, we review SimStudent, a system designed to aid authors in improving the quality of their tutors while simultaneously reducing authoring costs. This system works by inducing general production rule models from user demonstrations and feedback. We discuss results demonstrating that SimStudent learned models better fit student data than expert authored models and that it is eﬃcient to use. In addition, we provide an example of how a simple algebra tutor would be constructed using this paradigm. Lastly, we review future directions for this work."
